Bakery Bites

UX/UI Baking App Case Study

Bakery bites is a responsive baking app for peosple who suffer from severe food allergies and diseases, such as Crohn's, Irritable Bowel Syndrome, and Irritable Bowel Disease. The app is also for vegan and gluten-free users.

Purpose

To create a responsive web app for recipes that meet the needs of its user and solves the problems they are facing with existing recipe apps.

Roles

  • UI Designer
  • UX Researcher
  • Brand Design

Deliverables

  • Concept development
  • User flow
  • Wireframes
  • Style Guide
  • Mockups and Prototypes

Duration

2.5 months

Challenges

  • Users are seeking out recipes that don't mess with their gut health and make them stray from their diet.
  • Recently diagnosed users are seeking out community and accessible information related to their illness.
  • Creating a responsive web app that can be pulled up on desktop, mobile, or iPad.

Solutions

  • Providing easy recipes from creators who suffer from the same illness as them.
  • Having articles that allows recently diagnosed users to research more about their diagnosis from reliable sources.
  • Making sure the app is accessible and easy to browse in all settings.

User Personas

Three user personas were created based on early research and user interviews with candidates who fit the criteria. This process helped define the problems that needed solutions within other baking apps.

User Flow

Jobs to be done was identified and then an MVP document was created. The purpose of the MVP was to identify up to five key features of the app. The user flow was then the final touch in mapping out what the first iteration would like.

Low fidelity Wireframes

A crazy 8 process was used to draw out the first round of screens on paper. This allowed for quicker development. as first round of user testing was going to be on these paper prototypes.

Mood Board

To stay within a certain visual direction, a mood board was created to set the tone and feel of the app before laying out a style guide. At this point in the process, the user flow and features of the app was pretty set in stone. So the next step was to figure out what the style guide would look like before moving into mid-fidelity and final prototypes.

Style Guide

This guide lays out color scheme, typography, images, logo, and buttons.

Typography

Quicksand is the primary font used for copy text and logo. PT Sans is the secondary text that is only used for the headers.

Images

Images must be high-quality and focused on the dessert with no other distractions within the photo. It's important for the user to see a close up detail of what the product should look like once it's made.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

Remember earlier when I said the user flow of the app was set in stone? Well, that was before feedback was received from other designers after a second round of user testing with high-fidelity prototypes. The feedback was that while there's nothing wrong with bottom navigation, it doesn't really make sense for a responsive web app. So, a menu icon in the top header was implemented instead.

Final Designs

Future Iterations

Future iterations would include sharing options, message options to send creators a personal message, and a version of the app for creators only that allows them to upload recipes and keep in contact with the bakery bites team for questions and concerns.
