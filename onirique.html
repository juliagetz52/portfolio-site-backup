Onirique

UI Luxury Jewelry Design Case Study

Purpose

A web app where users can shop for luxury jewelry quickly and easily without the hassle of going into a physical store.

Roles

  • UI Designer
  • Brand Design
  • UX Researcher
  • Product Designer

Deliverables

  • Concept development
  • Brand guidelines
  • User flow
  • Wireframes
  • Prototype
  • User interface

Duration

1 month

Challenges

  • Allowing customers to place multiple items in a shopping cart, so that they can purchase more than one item at a time.
  • Providing a variety of payment options, so that the customer can choose the best payment method that suits their needs.
  • Must allow for customers to see the inventory without having to register.
  • Make navigation easy, so that users can filter and find what they need.

User Flow

As a customer, I need a simple checkout process that allows me to view multiple items in my cart.

Brand Guidelines

What does Onirique mean? Onirique is a French word that means dream-like or surreal in relation to an experience, person, or place. If Onirique's branding could be summed up into three words, it would be modern, elegant, and feminine.

Image Style

The image style for Onirique will require all images of jewelry to have a fabric background or at least an appearance of some fabric being draped near the jewelry. All images will be high-resolution and the jewelry will always be the focal point.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Quick ideation sketches were made to visualize layout and hierarchy with text and images. Several competitors were observed to gauge what of user navigation was most effective in the process of shopping for jewelry online.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

User Testing

User interviews allowed for room to see if mid-fidelity mockups were clear and needed any revisions upon moving forward. Only small edits were required as the navigation portion was commonly understood.

Participant 1

Positives: Clean and clear, very obvious what needs to be clicked and where things will lead. Critique: Make sure to include About, Contact Us, Refunds, etc. somewhere.

Participant 2

I walked through your prototype and it all looks really good to me. I think you definitely accomplished the objectives of viewing multiple items in cart very clearly and having a simple checkout process. I really like the grouping of all the express payment options together, and I appreciated the clear path ... > ... > at the top of the checkout showing the user how they got there and what they could navigate back to. Great job!

Participant 3

Positives: Instagram feature on home page is a fun social add on. Love the spacing indentation in the category menu because I can tell what the subcategories are. Checkout page is clear and familiar. Critique: I want to click the name of the category in category menu, not the arrow on the right. Align description of item text to the left of the item details page instead of centered.

Future Iterations

Future iterations of the app could include AI chat assistance, return and exchange screens, a repair service screen, and a message box directly implemented into the site.
